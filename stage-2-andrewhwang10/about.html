A platform that improves the flow of resources and volunteers to the places of most need.

Overview

Homelessness is a growing social issue in the city of Seattle. According to the Seattle.gov statement, there is a growing trend of people who are homeless in the city of Seattle. In 2018, the city of Seattle used a total budget of $77.6 million dollars on responding to the problem of homelessness. Despite this large sum of money being spent to address homelessness, the needs are steadily outpacing the resources used. As a social problem, homelessness is multifaceted and complex, both in addressing it and understanding it. The city of Seattle believes that some of the factors contributing to homelessness are: increased cost of living, income loss, prior criminal justice involvement, and education and employment gaps.

Around campus, there is significant presence of homeless populations. Regardless of the relevance and proximity of the problem of homelessness, university students aren't equipped with knowledge and tools of engagement, through the institution. Due to the lack of proper engagement within the institution, there is lack of proper knowledge of homelessness and ways to engage with it. Using technology, specifically web application, students and the university can have a centralized platform to engage with homelessness.

How this works

This web platform would allow users to donate resources to local shelters and volunteer groups. In addition, it would allow these shelter and groups to ask for specific resources they are in need of, which would help resources flow in the appropriate direction. A common problem students have is the need to spend dining resources by the end of the school year. This can also be addressed by allowing a way to use funds in a directed effort.
